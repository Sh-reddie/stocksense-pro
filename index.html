📖 Kelly Criterion Guide
How it works:
Position size = (Portfolio × Risk%) ÷ (Entry − Stop Loss)

Rule of thumb for aggressive traders:
• Never risk >2% per trade
• Never put >10% in one stock
• Keep 20-30% in cash for averaging down

For mid/small caps specifically:
• Reduce position size by 30-40% vs large caps
• Stop-loss is non-negotiable — illiquid stocks gap down
• Add in tranches: 50% entry, 25% on dip, 25% on confirmation

⚠ This is a mathematical tool, not a guarantee. Always manage your total portfolio risk.

💡 SIP Strategy Tips
For your aggressive mid/small cap portfolio:

Step-Up SIP: Increase monthly amount by 10% each year. At ₹10K/mo growing 10%/yr over 10 years at 15% CAGR → ₹1.3Cr vs ₹27L flat SIP.

Momentum-Based DCA: Deploy more when RSI < 40 (oversold), reduce when RSI > 70. Small caps mean-revert faster.

Satellite-Core split: 60% in large/midcap for stability, 40% in high-conviction small caps for alpha. Rebalance quarterly.

Tax-smart SIP: Set purchase date to 2nd of month — avoids monthly F&O expiry volatility on last Thursday.

✅ The biggest SIP mistake is stopping during corrections. Down 20%? Your SIP is now buying the same stock 20% cheaper.
